# Pronunciation- Consonants

### Do you know what a consonant is?

A consonant is a speech sound that is made by partially or completely blocking the flow of air as it passes through your mouth. To produce a consonant, your tongue, lips, teeth, or palate create an obstruction.  (palate /ˈpælət/ 腭 ; obstruction /əbˈstrʌkʃ(ə)n/ 堵塞 )

The opposite of a consonant is a vowel (A, E, I, O, U — and sometimes Y), where air flows freely without blockage. (blockage /ˈblɑːkɪdʒ/ 堵塞，阻塞 )

### Consonants can be voiced or unvoiced.

**Voiced sounds:** 

You vocal cords vibrate when you say these.

For example: z , b , v

**Unvoiced sounds:**

You vocal cords do not vibrate to say these.

For example: s, p, f

Put your hand on your throat to feel the vocal cords vibrate when you say them.

**Consonant pairs** are two sounds that are produced with the same mouth position but differ in voicing.**辅音对**是两种用相同口型发出、但在清浊上不同的声音。

Take the pair /p/ and /b/ for example. Your lips come together in the same way, but /p/ is voiceless (only air) and /b/ is voiced (with vocal cord vibration).以 /p/ 和 /b/ 这对为例。你的嘴唇闭合方式是一样的，但 /p/ 是清辅音（只有气流），而 /b/ 是浊辅音（声带振动）。
